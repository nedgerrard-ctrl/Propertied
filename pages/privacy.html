Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 July 2026  ·  Last reviewed: May 2026

Property Project Marketing Pty Ltd T/A Online Property Services and PPM Online Projects ("PPM", "we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of individuals who interact with our business. This Privacy Policy sets out how we collect, hold, use and disclose personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

1. Who We Are

PPM is a licensed real estate agency (Licence No. 074846L) operating in Victoria, Australia. ABN 99 162 429 558. Registered office: Level 7, 570 St Kilda Road, Melbourne, Victoria 3004. This policy applies to all PPM operations, including ppmonlineprojects.com.au and all associated communications.

2. What Personal Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Identity information: name, date of birth, passport or driver's licence details (for AML/CTF verification)
  • Contact information: email address, phone number, mailing address
  • Financial information: stated budget, source of funds (where required for AML/CTF compliance), bank account details for disbursements (property management clients)
  • Property information: details of properties you own, lease, or are interested in purchasing
  • Communication records: emails, phone calls, meeting notes and enquiry forms submitted via our Website
  • Website usage data: IP address, browser type, pages visited (via cookies — see Section 14)

3. How We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information directly from you when you: submit an enquiry via our Website; contact us by phone or email; attend an inspection, open home or private appointment; sign an agency agreement or property management agreement; or engage us in any capacity as a client. We may also collect information from third parties such as developers, solicitors, conveyancers, lenders and government bodies where necessary to facilitate a transaction.

4. Why We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information for the following primary purposes:

  • To provide real estate sales, property management and advisory services to you
  • To fulfil obligations under the Estate Agents Act 1980 (Vic) and associated regulations
  • To comply with AML/CTF obligations under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (Cth)
  • To communicate with you about properties, market updates, enquiries and transactions
  • To process and manage rental payments, trust account disbursements and maintenance requests
  • To respond to enquiries and provide information requested by you

5. Marketing Communications

With your consent, we may use your contact information to send you property market updates, new project alerts and information about PPM's services. You may opt out of marketing communications at any time by following the unsubscribe link in any email we send, or by contacting us directly. Opting out of marketing communications will not affect our ability to contact you in relation to an active transaction or existing engagement.

6. Disclosure of Personal Information

We may disclose your personal information to:

  • Developers, vendors and their representatives, where necessary to facilitate a purchase or sale
  • Solicitors, conveyancers and settlement agents
  • Property maintenance contractors and tradespeople (property management clients)
  • Financial institutions (where required for trust account disbursements)
  • AUSTRAC, Consumer Affairs Victoria, VCAT, or other regulatory bodies (as required by law)
  • Our technology and software service providers, under confidentiality obligations

We do not sell personal information to third parties. We do not share personal information with overseas entities unless required by law or with your express consent.

7. Overseas Disclosure

PPM does not routinely transfer personal information outside Australia. Where we use cloud-based software services, we take reasonable steps to ensure that overseas providers handle personal information in a manner consistent with the Australian Privacy Principles. We will not disclose personal information to an overseas recipient without your consent or without being satisfied that the recipient is bound by similar privacy protections.

8. How We Hold and Protect Personal Information

Personal information is held securely in electronic and physical form. Electronic records are protected by access controls, password authentication and encryption where appropriate. Physical documents containing personal information are held in locked storage. We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification or disclosure.

9. Retention of Personal Information

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, and as required by law. Under the Estate Agents Act 1980 (Vic), certain trust account and transaction records must be retained for a minimum of seven years. AML/CTF records must be retained for a minimum of seven years under the AML/CTF Rules. After applicable retention periods, personal information will be securely destroyed or de-identified.

10. Access to Your Personal Information

You have the right to request access to personal information that PPM holds about you. We will respond to access requests within 30 days. In some circumstances, we may be unable to provide access — for example, where doing so would unreasonably impact the privacy of another person, or where exemptions under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) apply. If we decline an access request, we will provide written reasons.

11. Correction of Personal Information

If you believe that personal information we hold about you is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, irrelevant or misleading, you may request that we correct it. We will respond to correction requests within 30 days. If we decline to correct information, you may request that we attach a statement noting your view.

12. Complaints

If you believe PPM has mishandled your personal information or breached the Australian Privacy Principles, you may lodge a complaint by contacting us at info@ppmproperty.com.au. We will acknowledge your complaint within five business days and respond substantively within 30 days. If you are not satisfied with our response, you may lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au.

13. Trust Account Information

Financial information held in connection with PPM's trust account (including landlord and tenant payment records, bond amounts and disbursement schedules) is handled with the highest degree of confidentiality. This information is disclosed only to the relevant parties to the transaction (owner, tenant, authorised contractors) or as required by the Estate Agents Act 1980 (Vic), the Residential Tenancies Act 1997 (Vic) or by a court or tribunal order.

14. Cookies and Website Analytics

Our Website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to improve the user experience and analyse website traffic. Cookies are small data files placed on your device. We use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with our Website. You can control or disable cookies through your browser settings, though doing so may affect the functionality of parts of the Website. We do not use cookies to collect personally identifiable information without your consent.

15. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed at persons under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a person under 18 without parental consent, we will take steps to delete that information.

16. Third-Party Websites

Our Website may contain links to third-party websites. This Privacy Policy does not apply to those websites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

17. Changes to This Policy

PPM may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Updated versions will be published on our Website with a revised "Last reviewed" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our Website or services after a change to this policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms.

18. Governing Law

This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of the Commonwealth of Australia and the State of Victoria. Any dispute arising in connection with this policy shall be subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Victoria.

19. Contact Us

For all privacy enquiries, access and correction requests, and complaints, please contact PPM's Privacy Officer at:

Email: info@ppmproperty.com.au
