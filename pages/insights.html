Policy Update · May 2026

The 2026 Federal Budget has changed the investment landscape.

Published May 2026  ·  PPM Research  ·  budget.gov.au

"The 2026 Federal Budget has restructured the tax treatment of residential property investment in ways that materially favour new builds over established housing. This is not a small adjustment. For investors, the implications are significant and time-sensitive."

What has changed

The Treasurer's Budget night announcement on 12 May 2026 introduced two material changes to residential property investment taxation, effective from 1 July 2027.

First, negative gearing will be quarantined for established property — meaning investors in existing housing stock will no longer be able to offset rental losses against other taxable income. This treatment is preserved, unchanged, for new builds: apartments, townhouses and houses contracted as new constructions.

Second, CGT treatment is modified. Investors in established property will move to an indexation model for the capital gains discount. Investors in new builds retain the ability to elect the existing 50% discount model — a structurally superior position in most holding scenarios.

Grandfathering — what is protected

Contracts exchanged before 7:30pm on 12 May 2026 (Budget night) are fully grandfathered under existing rules. If you already hold an established property or exchanged contracts before that time, the rules do not apply to you retrospectively.

Properties contracted between now and 30 June 2027 — provided they qualify as new builds — will receive the full new-build tax treatment when the changes take effect from 1 July 2027. This represents a meaningful window of opportunity.

What this means for Melbourne buyers

Off-the-plan apartments and townhouses in Melbourne are the primary asset class positioned to benefit from these changes. They are new builds by definition. They qualify for preserved negative gearing treatment. And they qualify for the retained 50% CGT discount election.

This structural advantage did not exist — or was not recognised — twelve months ago. It now exists in legislation. For investors who have been sitting on the sidelines, the calculus has changed.

PPM has been sourcing, vetting and selling Melbourne off-the-plan property for 13 years. We know which projects are genuinely investable — and which are not. If you want to understand what is available in the current market, speak to us before the window closes.

Useful source

The full Budget papers, including the housing measures fact sheet, are available at budget.gov.au. PPM recommends clients review these materials in conjunction with advice from their tax professional.
